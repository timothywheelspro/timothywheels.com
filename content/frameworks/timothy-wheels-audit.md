## 1) Executive Summary (30 seconds)

Timothy Wheels has strong product fundamentals but is constrained by trust gaps and conversion friction. The current site underperforms relative to its premium price point, likely suppressing conversion rates. The highest-leverage fixes are trust signals, checkout friction removal, and platform modernization. These changes should be treated as revenue unlocks, not cosmetic improvements.

## 2) The Audit Framework (Reusable)

### A. Trust Architecture

**Question:** Do buyers feel safe spending $1,000+?

**Signals to verify:** Reviews, certifications, social proof, transparent returns, founder story, visible contact info.

**Real-world test:** Would I trust this brand if I'd never heard of them?

**Timothy Wheels finding:** No - zero reviews visible above fold. Warranty terms buried. No founder story. Contact info footer-only. A $1,200 purchase requires more trust than the current site provides.

---

### B. Conversion Friction

**Question:** Does the path from interest -> purchase feel effortless?

**Signals to verify:** Guest checkout, CTA clarity, product confidence, shipping/returns visibility.

**Real-world test:** Can a first-time visitor complete purchase in under 3 minutes without confusion?

**Timothy Wheels finding:** Mandatory account creation adds friction at highest-intent moment. CTAs are small and generic ("Add to Cart" without benefit reinforcement). Shipping/returns require navigation to find - not visible on product page.

---

### C. Platform Capability

**Question:** Can the brand iterate fast and test improvements?

**Signals to verify:** Modern e-commerce stack, app ecosystem, A/B testing, mobile performance.

**Real-world test:** Can the team ship a homepage test in under 24 hours?

**Timothy Wheels finding:** Template-bound site limits iteration velocity. No visible A/B testing infrastructure. Mobile performance adequate but not optimized for thumb-zone navigation.

---

### D. Authority and Content Flywheel

**Question:** Does content build trust and capture demand?

**Signals to verify:** SEO strategy, customer stories, technical education, video proof.

**Real-world test:** Does the site rank for "[product category] reviews" or "[product] vs [competitor]"?

**Timothy Wheels finding:** Minimal content strategy. No comparison pages. No buyer's guide. No video showing product in use. Organic acquisition left on the table.

## 3) Findings (High Impact)

### 1. Trust is the primary bottleneck

High-ticket wheels require maximum credibility. The current site lacks strong, persistent trust signals. No reviews. No certifications displayed. No social proof. No user-generated content.

**Impact:** Suppresses conversion and increases abandoned carts. Buyers who want to purchase talk themselves out of it.

---

### 2. Checkout and UX introduce friction

Mandatory account creation and dated UX patterns reduce completion. The final step - where intent is highest - adds unnecessary barriers.

**Impact:** Direct loss of revenue at the final step. Industry data suggests 20-30% of cart abandonment ties to forced account creation.

---

### 3. The platform limits growth velocity

Site feels template-bound with limited iteration power. No visible testing infrastructure. Changes require development cycles instead of quick experiments.

**Impact:** Slower testing, lower conversion improvement velocity, weak tooling for optimization.

---

## 4) Revenue Levers (Prioritized)

### Phase 1 - Trust + Conversion (Quick Wins)

*Timeline: Week 1-2 | Expected lift: 15-25% conversion improvement*

- Implement a review system (Judge.me/Yotpo)
- Enable guest checkout
- Add trust badges, warranty, shipping and return summaries on every product page
- Revamp About/Story page with human narrative + workshop credibility
- Add founder photo and direct contact option

---

### Phase 2 - UX + Product Discovery

*Timeline: Week 3-4 | Expected lift: 10-15% additional conversion improvement*

- Add filters by size, brake type, weight, price
- Increase CTA size + add benefit-driven copy ("Add to Cart" -> "Get Yours - Free Shipping")
- Improve mobile tap targets and checkout clarity
- Add product comparison functionality
- Implement "recently viewed" and "customers also bought"

---

### Phase 3 - Authority + Acquisition

*Timeline: Month 2-3 | Expected lift: 20-40% traffic increase, improved conversion from organic*

- Content strategy: wheel care, tech deep dives, buyer guides
- Influencer reviews / YouTube partnerships
- Email lead magnet: "Wheelchair Wheel Buyer's Guide"
- Comparison pages: "Timothy Wheels vs. [Competitor]"
- Customer story videos (social proof + SEO)
